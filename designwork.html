RateMyLandlord Figma Prototype



Overview:

Ann Arbor’s competitive housing market and a lack of transparency leave University of Michigan students vulnerable to predatory landlord practices including high waitlist fees and forced early signing. Transfer, international, and low-income students are especially impacted, with few trusted sources beyond word-of-mouth or online forums.


Project Approach:

Our team proposed a platform for UM students to anonymously review landlords and share experiences, helping future tenants make informed housing choices. Pre-launch survey outreach validated student demand for a trusted information source and an initial static prorotype was developed.


Core Features:

  • Verified reviews (UM uniqname/email), property/location data, and anonymous posting for privacy protection
  • Community reporting to counter misinformation with manual review and clear disclaimers
  • Cloud-based backend for secure data handling

Design Impact and Considerations:

  • Reduces exploitation by creating housing markey transparency
  • Proects users with anonymity features and legal safeguards
  • Enables ongoing analysis of housing trends, pricing, and common landlord issues

Breaking the Cycle: Addressing Menstrual Product Insecurity Among College Students

Overview:

Many college students struggle to afford menstrual products, and current university initiatives such as stocking supplies in restrooms often do not meet students’ varied and ongoing needs. This project collected student perspectives to assess how effective these approaches are in addressing period poverty.


Process:

  • Recruited 5 menstruating college students from multiple institutions for interviews
  • Used an affinity diagram to analyze experiences and challenges with access
  • Ensured participant consent and data privacy throughout

Key Findings:

  • Students frequently run out of period products while on campus, leading to stress and discomfort
  • Existing solutions (like coin dispensers or unstocked baskets) are insufficient, especially for varied product sizing and unpredictable needs
  • Most participants want free, reliably stocked period products and rely on student groups when university solutions fall short
  • The issue is common and widely discussed among students

Deliverables:

  • Created a persona based on participants with heavier periods, highlighting the urgent need for reliable access
  • Developed a storyboard illustrating a typical student’s experience with inadequate product availability and its effects on well-being

Lessons Learned:

  • Future research would benefit from a broader participant pool, especially those most dependent on publicly provided products
  • Learned the importance of unbiased, open-ended interview questions to gather more objective insights
