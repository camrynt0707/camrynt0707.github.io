RateMyLandlord Figma Prototype

Team: Camryn Toth and collaborators

Project Overview

Our team addressed the lack of transparency and tenant protections in Ann Arbor’s highly competitive housing market. University of Michigan students face predatory landlord practices, such as steep waitlist fees and forced early signing. This project focused on designing a platform for students to anonymously review landlords and share experiences, empowering future tenants with reliable housing information.

Problem Statement / Challenge

Student renters struggle to find trustworthy information about Ann Arbor landlords. High demand drives rent up, and current university and community solutions do not meet diverse student needs. Transfer, international, and low-income students are disproportionately affected.

Process & Methods

  • Surveyed UM students to validate demand and collect pre-product data
  • Developed a static Figma prototype of the review platform
  • Designed system for verified, anonymous reviews and secure data handling

Solution & Features

  • Verified, anonymous posting using UM uniqname/email and property/location data
  • Cloud backend for secure data storage
  • Community reporting and moderation for reliable information
  • Legal/ethical privacy protections for users

Lessons Learned

  • Importance of robust privacy and moderation to ensure safe and trustworthy reviews
  • Need to represent diverse student identities in product design and prototyping
