RateMyLandlord Figma Prototype

Co-contributors: Callum Holley, Janellyn Woo, Jonathan Kim, Thejo Akkoor

Overview

Ann Arbor’s competitive housing market and a lack of transparency leave University of Michigan students vulnerable to predatory landlord practices including high waitlist fees and forced early signing. Transfer, international, and low-income students are especially impacted, with few trusted sources beyond word-of-mouth or online forums.

Project Approach

Our team proposed a platform for UM students to anonymously review landlords and share experiences, helping future tenants make informed housing choices. Pre-launch survey outreach validated student demand for a trusted information source and an initial static prototype was developed.

Core Features

  • Users can post anonymously, with verification through their UM uniqname or email, along with details about the property or location
  • Community-driven reporting and moderation help ensure the accuracy and trustworthiness of information
  • Search functionality allows users to find details about particular landlords or properties
  • Landlords receive ratings on their communication, reliability, and quality, along with an overall 5-star score and a list of the properties they oversee.

Design Impact and Considerations

  • Reduces exploitation by creating housing market transparency
  • Protects users with anonymity features and legal safeguards
  • Enables ongoing analysis of housing trends, pricing, and common landlord issues
