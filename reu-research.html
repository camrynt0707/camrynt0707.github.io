REU Research: UX for Marginalized Online Daters

Project Overview

  • Title: Designing & Analyzing UX for Marginalized Online Daters (Deaf, Hard of Hearing, Visually Impaired, Autistic)
  • Timeline: Summer REU Program, University of Michigan–Flint
  • Role: Undergraduate Researcher
  • Team: 2 undergrads, several grad students, lead professor

Problem Statement / Challenge

Online dating presents unique challenges for Deaf, Hard of Hearing, Visually Impaired, and Autistic adults, especially around intention & consent in digital interactions. How do marginalized users experience giving and receiving sexual consent online—and what solutions can improve their experiences?

Process & Methods

  • Project 1: Analyzed past experiences of D/HH/VI online daters via reflexive thematic analysis of interview/survey data.
  • Project 2: Survey design, data collection, preliminary analysis for Autistic online daters.
  • Project 3: Participatory design and survey for autistic adults about consent, technology solutions, and hypothetical scenarios.

Solutions & Deliverables

  • Thematic maps showing common issues/barriers in online consent.
  • Insights on desired technology features to support consent-awareness.
  • Example survey questions and synthesized user journey narratives.

Impact & Outcomes

  • Identified unique needs per group (privacy, communication barriers, etc.).
  • Recommendations for more accessible design features in dating platforms.
  • Presented findings to research mentors & team; contributed to ongoing study.

Lessons Learned

  • Flexible project management skills (juggled three shifting projects).
  • Learned rigorous qualitative analysis.
  • Grew communication/collaboration working with multi-disciplinary teams.
