Student Perspectives in AI Education Policy: Rebuilding Trust and Collaboration

This paper investigates the concrete impacts of AI regulation in higher education by analyzing student surveys, interviews, and educator perspectives. It explains how common policies such as AI bans, detection software, and assignments designed to resist AI can undermine student trust and damage teacher-student relationships. Using mixed-methods research and ethical analysis, the study highlights key shortcomings in current regulations and shows how the lack of student input in policy-making leads to increased distrust. Case examples, including Oak National Academy and College Unbound, illustrate collaborative policy models where student voices help shape AI strategy. The paper concludes with actionable recommendations for developing transparent and student-informed AI policies that foster ethical, effective, and trusting academic environments.


  • Reviewed current AI regulations and policies in higher education
  • Analyzed survey and interview data on student perspectives and trust
  • Assessed policy models for ethical and collaborative AI integration

Unpacking Duolingo’s AI: Ethical Concerns and Effectiveness in Language Education

This case study analyzes Duolingo’s use of artificial intelligence in language learning. It highlights ethical concerns over workforce layoffs, reduced attention to less popular languages, and the limits of AI in capturing cultural and emotional nuance. The paper calls for Duolingo to adopt clear ethical policies, equitable resource allocation, and transparency, positioning AI as a complement to human educators rather than a replacement.


  • Explored ethical considerations in corporate AI adoption
  • Applied critical thinking to evaluate business practices and social effects
  • Communicated evidence-based recommendations for technology policy
