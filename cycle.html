Breaking the Cycle: Addressing Menstrual Product Insecurity

Project Lead: Camryn Toth

Project Overview

This project explored the challenge of period poverty on college campuses. Many students struggle to afford menstrual products, and university provisions—such as stocking restrooms—often do not meet the variety and consistency needed for ongoing access.

Problem Statement / Challenge

Menstruating college students face financial hurdles in accessing sufficient period products. Existing university solutions, like coin dispensers and occasional free products, are often sparse, unreliable, and lack diversity in product types.

Process & Methods

  • Recruited students from multiple institutions for in-depth interviews
  • Conducted affinity diagram analysis to synthesize major themes and needs
  • Ensured privacy and thorough consent with all participants

Key Findings

  • Students often lack access to products on campus, resulting in stress and distraction
  • Current university provisions fail to meet diverse needs and product preferences
  • Free, consistently available period products are broadly desired

Deliverables

  • Persona modeling based on students with unpredictable cycles and frequent need for products
  • Storyboard depicting a real student scenario navigating period product insecurity

Lessons Learned

  • Future interviews should include more diverse institutions and students with greater dependence on public products
  • Enhancing objectivity through open-ended, unbiased research questions improves insights
