Campus Crawlz: A Centralized Event Discovery App

Project Overview

In my EECS 498 Social Computing course, I worked in a four-person team to develop VoiceMail, a web-based system designed to bring a sense of intentionality and emotional nuance back to digital voice communication. Inspired by the deliberate pacing of traditional letters, our app helped users cultivate deeper relationships despite the interruptions of everyday life. I collaborated with my team to create a carefully planned design and build the prototype, then coordinated and analyzed user studies to assess our platform’s impact on intimacy and communication frequency.

Project Highlights

  • Design: Led the creation of user flows and wireframes in Figma, emphasizing purposeful, asynchronous communication.
  • Development: Built a functional web prototype with React, TypeScript, Node.js, and Firebase, implementing secure user management with Firestore.
  • Evaluation: Deployed pre- and post-surveys and conducted participant interviews to assess sociotechnical impacts, analyzed qualitative feedback with thematic mapping, and ran Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Tests on survey data.
  • Teamwork: Partnered with my teammates throughout the design and development process, combining iteration with attentive user feedback.

Impact and Outcomes

  • Users experienced less social pressure and felt able to communicate with more intention than on typical social media platforms.
  • The simplified, distraction-free design encouraged focused, one-to-one voice messaging that felt warm and personal.
  • Both distant and close relationships benefited from deeper connection and more thoughtful conversations.
  • Some onboarding challenges highlighted the need for clearer guidance and norm-setting for users new to the platform.

Lessons Learned

  • Practiced balancing qualitative and quantitative evaluation for a robust understanding of user impact.
  • Developed strong communication and project management skills within a multidisciplinary team.
  • Sharpened the ability to iterate on user feedback and quickly adapt the product to real user needs.

Tools and Skills Used

Figma, React, TypeScript, Node.js, Firebase, Firestore, thematic analysis, Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test, user interviews/surveys.
