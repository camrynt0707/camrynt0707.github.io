Campus Crawlz: A Centralized Event Discovery App

Co-contributors: Rhea Bhanushali, Leena Khan, and Lanny Lo

Project Overview

As part of my EECS 493: User Interface Development course, I collaborated with a team to research and prototype a centralized student event discovery system. We addressed the widespread frustration that students experience when navigating fragmented information about campus and off-campus activities. Through extensive interviews and user testing, our team designed an app to streamline event visibility, simplify RSVP processes, and provide clear attendance transparency. Our goal was to empower students to participate in more social opportunities and reduce the stress of relying on scattered chats, flyers, and emails.

Project Highlights

  • User Research: Identified fragmentation, lack of RSVP clarity, notification overload, and transportation barriers as major pain points
  • Prototype Development: Created and iterated a Figma-based mockup to aggregate event listings, show attendee lists, and offer clear RSVP features
  • Usability Testing Conducted user tests, collected feedback, and refined the design to maximize accessibility and ease-of-use

Affinity Diagram With Miro


Impact and Outcomes

  • The prototype enabled users to easily discover, RSVP to, and track campus events, fulfilling key needs around event transparency and promotion
  • Changes to RSVP and friend features improved user experience, with better feedback and clearer event tracking, though additional work is needed for feedback clarity
  • Friend search and following functionalities were well received, making it easier for users to keep up with relevant events and contacts
  • Some limitations remain, such as lacking a share/repost feature for broader event promotion and an absent keyboard for messaging, which restricts full engagement

Lessons Learned

  • Cultivated empathy for diverse student challenges, notably those related to transportation and social anxiety
  • Practiced iterative, user-centered design from initial research through final prototyping
  • Gained proficiency in communicating research findings and recommendations to multiple stakeholders

Tools and Skills Used

Figma, user interviews, usability testing, stakeholder communication, need-finding, iterative prototyping.
