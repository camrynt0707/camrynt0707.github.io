REU Research - UX for Marginalized Online Daters

Project Overview

  • Title: Designing & Analyzing UX for Marginalized Online Daters (Deaf, Hard of Hearing, Visually Impaired, Autistic)
  • Timeline: Summer REU Program, University of Michigan–Flint
  • Role: Undergraduate Researcher; collaborated with another undergrad, grad students, and a faculty mentor.
  • Team: 2 undergrads, several grad students, lead professor.

Problem Statement / Challenge

Online dating presents unique challenges for Deaf, Hard of Hearing, Visually Impaired, and Autistic adults, especially around intention & consent in digital interactions.

Process & Methods

  • Project 1: Reflexive thematic analysis for Deaf/Hard of Hearing/Visually Impaired daters.
  • Project 2: Survey design & thematic analysis for Autistic online daters.
  • Project 3: Participatory design: surveyed autistic adults about consent and technology solutions.

Solutions & Deliverables

  • Thematic maps of barriers/issues.
  • Technology feature insights for consent-awareness.
  • Example survey and synthesized user journeys.

Impact & Outcomes

  • Identified needs & design recommendations for platforms.
  • Presented findings; contributed to ongoing study.

Lessons Learned

  • Flexible project management across three related projects.
  • Rigorous qualitative analysis & multidisciplinary teamwork.

EECS 498: VoiceMail – Intentional Voice Communication Platform

Project Overview

  • Title: VoiceMail – Creating Intentional Voice Communication
  • Timeline: EECS 498 Capstone Project
  • Role: UX Designer, Developer (team of 4)
  • Tech Stack: Figma, React & TypeScript, Node.js & Firebase, Firestore

Problem Statement / Challenge

How can digital voice communication foster deeper, more intentional connections despite obstacles of distance and time?

Process & Methods

  • Designed user flows/wireframes in Figma, inspired by letter-writing.
  • Built a full-stack web app (React, Node.js, Firebase).
  • Ran qualitative (surveys/interviews, thematic analysis) and quantitative tests (usage metrics, Likert results, Wilcoxon stats).

Solutions & Deliverables

  • Web app prototype, letter-style interface.
  • Evaluation summary: usage graphs, quotes/themes.

Impact & Outcomes

  • Users reported more meaningful voice communication.
  • Increase in message volume and emotional nuance.
  • Positive design feedback from participants.

Lessons Learned

  • Balanced qualitative & quantitative evaluation.
  • Iterative teamwork in design/dev.

EECS 493: Centralized Event Discovery App

Project Overview

  • Title: Unified Social Event Discovery – Empowering Student Participation
  • Timeline: EECS 493 Project
  • Role: UX Designer/Researcher
  • Tools: Figma, user testing/interviews

Problem Statement / Challenge

Students miss events due to fragmented info sources and inefficient planning, especially for off-campus events.

Process & Methods

  • Research: Interviews about event awareness, notification clutter, attendee visibility, RSVP systems, transportation.
  • Design: Figma prototype for centralized event feed/RSVP/maps.
  • Testing: Usability testing, iterative UI improvements.

Solutions & Deliverables

  • Prototype screenshots: event feeds, RSVP, filters.
  • User journey diagrams.
  • Design recommendations for student orgs.

Impact & Outcomes

  • Students reported greater event awareness and confidence in participation.
  • Recommendations for U-M and student orgs.

Lessons Learned

  • Practiced user-centered research & empathy for diverse needs.
  • Improved UI through iterative prototyping & testing.
